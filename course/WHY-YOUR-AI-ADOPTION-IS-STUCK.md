# Why Your AI Adoption Is Stuck

## Maven course brief — v0.1

**Subtitle:** Use the Adaptive Adoption Friction Audit to diagnose what is blocking value and build a focused 90-day response.

**Format:** Fully asynchronous, multi-day course. No required live sessions, office hours, community participation, or instructor feedback.

**Core resource:** The AI Adoption Friction Audit hosted on paulgibbonsadvisory.com.

## Learner promise

By the end of the course, the learner will have:

1. defined one AI initiative precisely enough to diagnose;
2. completed the eight-domain Friction Audit;
3. distinguished symptoms from plausible mechanisms;
4. tested the strength of the evidence behind the result;
5. identified the highest-priority friction pattern; and
6. drafted a focused 90-day response.

## Intended learner

- AI transformation and adoption leaders
- business-unit sponsors of AI initiatives
- product and programme leaders moving pilots into operations
- change, organisational-development, and learning practitioners
- consultants supporting AI implementation
- functional leaders responsible for human–AI workflow redesign

## Course structure

### Day 1 — Define the initiative and the adoption problem

**Outcome:** A bounded initiative brief.

Lessons:

1. Why “AI adoption” is too broad to diagnose
2. The initiative, workflow, user group, intended value, and observed problem
3. Adoption is changed work—not access, training completion, or tool usage alone
4. Exercise: complete the initiative brief

**Paul video:** 12–15 minutes across two short segments.

### Day 2 — Diagnose the friction

**Outcome:** A completed Friction Audit.

Lessons:

1. The eight friction domains
2. How to rate present conditions rather than aspirations
3. Consequence versus friction
4. Behavioral evidence: anecdote, artifact, observed behavior
5. Exercise: complete the website Audit

**Paul video:** 15–18 minutes across three short segments.

### Day 3 — Interpret the pattern

**Outcome:** A defensible working diagnosis.

Lessons:

1. Why there is no grand maturity score
2. Reading domain patterns and interactions
3. Rational and protective friction
4. What evidence would disconfirm the leading explanation?
5. Case exercise: compare a training problem with a workflow or incentive problem

**Paul video:** 15–18 minutes across three short segments.

### Day 4 — Build the response

**Outcome:** A provisional 90-day intervention plan.

Lessons:

1. Match interventions to mechanisms
2. Assign ownership and decision rights
3. Define behavioral signals and evidence
4. Use stop, adapt, continue, and scale checkpoints
5. Exercise: complete the 90-day planning canvas

**Paul video:** 15–18 minutes across three short segments.

## Total learner experience

Approximately 3–4 hours, completed over four suggested days or at the learner’s own pace.

Expected original Paul recording: approximately 60–70 minutes in short segments.

## Product boundary

The course teaches responsible use of a structured organizational diagnostic. It does not certify an organization, provide a psychometric assessment, perform a legal or technical risk audit, or include personal consulting from Paul.
