# Friction Courage

**The failure mode isn't stupidity — it's cowardice. They know the silos are broken. They don't act.**

*Archetype: THE LIBERATOR*

## The Claim

The failure mode isn't stupidity — it's cowardice. They know the silos are broken. They don't act.

## Observable Behaviors

### Lead Self — Habits & Practices

**Purge Self-Frictions** — Pro-level self and time management as leadership prerequisite — ruthless, not incremental. The exec who can't manage their own calendar, who says yes to everything, whose inbox runs them — that person cannot credibly liberate an organization from structural friction.

**Courage Inventory** — Monthly honest audit: "What friction do I know is broken that I haven't acted on — in my own work AND in the organization?"

### Lead Others — Behaviors

**Break the Structures, Love the People** — Welch-level willingness to destroy unproductive structures with genuine care for the humans inside them. The leader who breaks structures AND breaks people is a sociopath. The leader who loves people but won't break structures is a coward. Gut the silo, protect the person. Tell legal to take a hike, take the lawyer to lunch.

**Naming the Cowardice** — Calling out organizational cowardice — including your own — as a leadership act.

### Lead System — Architecture

**The Dual Mandate — Protect AND Expand** — The protect/break ratio is not fixed — it's calibrated to ambition. Low growth ambition: protect heavily, optimize incrementally. High growth ambition: break things, accept some casualties, move fast.

**Friction Audit Protocol** — Systematic mapping of deliberate vs. accidental frictions. Deliberate frictions (ethical review, security checks) are load-bearing — protect them. Accidental frictions (legacy approval chains, silo-driven handoffs, compliance theater) are structural debt — destroy them.
