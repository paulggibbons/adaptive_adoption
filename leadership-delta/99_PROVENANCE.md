# Leadership Delta — Provenance

## Layer Identity
**The Torque** — what leaders do differently. Seven dimensions of the measurable gap between current leadership capability and what AI-era leadership demands. Not a competency model (static) but a delta (dynamic, assessable, closable).

## Why "Delta"
Implies measurable change — the gap between where leadership capability is and where it needs to be. Every dimension is assessable: observable behaviors, not personality traits. The delta closes through practice, not training.

## The Seven Dimensions

1. **The 10x Narrative** — replacing fixed "future state" vision with adaptive, directional narrative
2. **Active Modeling** — an AI sponsor who doesn't use AI is a contradiction
3. **Innovation Climate** — creating the conditions for experimentation and learning
4. **Friction Courage** — removing structural obstacles rather than just "supporting" change
5. **Trust Calibration** — modeling both appropriate trust and appropriate skepticism
6. **Ethical Leadership** — practicing moral reasoning about AI, not delegating it to compliance
7. **Systems Orchestration** — seeing and acting on the whole system, not just the parts

## Intellectual Lineage

| Tradition | Key Source | Contribution |
|---|---|---|
| Adaptive Leadership | Heifetz & Linsky, *Leadership on the Line* (2002) | Mobilizing people to tackle tough challenges; distinguishing technical from adaptive work |
| Transformational Leadership | Burns (1978); Bass & Riggio (2006) | Idealized influence, intellectual stimulation, individualized consideration |
| Psychological Safety | Edmondson, *The Fearless Organization* (2019); Hoffman et al. (2025) | Precondition for trust calibration, error disclosure, experimentation |
| Complexity Leadership | Uhl-Bien & Arena, *Complexity Leadership* (2018) | Enabling rather than directing in complex adaptive systems |
| Ethical Leadership | Gibbons, *The Science of Organizational Change* (2015) | Ethics as leadership behavior, not department |
