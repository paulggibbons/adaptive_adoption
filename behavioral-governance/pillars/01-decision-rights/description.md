# Decision Rights

**"If someone wants to spin up a small pilot, who do they have to ask? If the answer is 'three committees and nine months,' they'll build in the dark."**

## The Claim

Decision Rights govern who is authorized to approve, launch, modify, or terminate AI initiatives — and how rapidly those decisions can be made. The dimension rejects static RACI matrices in favor of dynamic, context-sensitive authority structures. Human oversight is calibrated to demonstrated AI reliability, not blanket caution. The model draws from holacracy's distributed authority rather than traditional hierarchy: authority flows to demonstrated competence and proximity to the problem, not positional rank.

When decision rights are miscalibrated — too centralized, too slow, too opaque — the predictable consequence is Shadow AI proliferation. People who cannot get permission will build without it. The governance failure is not disobedience; it is a rational response to a system that cannot keep pace with the velocity of AI capability change.
