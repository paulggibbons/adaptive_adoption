# Governance Intelligence

**"The measurement system that governs the governance. The Five Dials™. Meta-governance — are the guardrails themselves working?"**

## The Claim

Governance Intelligence is the meta-governance dimension — the system that measures whether governance itself is functioning. It provides the board-level readout on AI governance maturity through The Five Dials™ (Utilization Depth, Capability Expansion Rate, Trust Stability, Iteration Velocity, Leadership Delta) and the Three-Layer Assessment architecture.

Most governance frameworks assume their own effectiveness. Governance Intelligence refuses that assumption. It asks: Do the policies we wrote actually change behavior? Are decision rights being exercised as designed, or are they being bypassed? Is agent authority calibrated correctly, or are people overriding it? The Three-Layer Assessment — self-report, evidence, and behavioral observation — exists precisely because self-report alone is unreliable. What people say they do, what the system logs show, and what an observer would see in practice are frequently three different things. Governance Intelligence is the dimension that detects and closes those gaps.
