# 1st-Derivative Talent

**"Not what skills people have — the rate at which they acquire new ones. And at CAIO level: is the AI talent pipeline bleeding the best?"**

## The Claim

1st-Derivative Talent measures the rate of capability acquisition, not the capability inventory. The metaphor is mathematical: the first derivative of a function measures its rate of change. An organization where 80% of employees use AI but whose adoption rate has plateaued is less adaptive than one at 40% adoption with an accelerating learning curve.

This dimension governs dynamic role architecture rather than static competency models. Job descriptions, skill frameworks, and training curricula that are fixed at annual intervals cannot keep pace with AI capability shifts that occur weekly. The dimension also addresses the CAIO-level strategic question: is the organization's AI talent pipeline attracting, retaining, and developing the people it needs — or is it bleeding its best to competitors who move faster? The governing conditions are Epistemically Different (AI skills are categorically unlike prior technology skills — they require new learning modes) and Velocity (the half-life of an AI skill is measured in months, not years).

## Conditions

- Epistemically Different
- Velocity
