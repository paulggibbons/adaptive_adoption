# Strategic Coherence

**"Strategic coherence is as important as strategic excellence." — Paul Gibbons**

## The Claim

Strategic Coherence measures the alignment between AI strategy, business strategy, and governance. An organization may have an excellent AI strategy and an excellent business strategy that contradict each other — or a governance framework that impedes both. Coherence is the connective tissue.

The dimension addresses two governing conditions. Complication: as AI initiatives multiply across business units, the interactions between them generate emergent complexity that no single strategy document anticipates. Stack Sprawl: the proliferation of AI tools, platforms, and vendors creates a fragmented technology landscape where individual choices are locally rational but collectively incoherent. Strategic Coherence requires a governance mechanism that surfaces misalignment before it becomes entrenched — not a one-time strategic planning exercise, but a continuous sensing function that detects when AI investments, organizational priorities, and governance guardrails have drifted apart.

## Conditions

- Complication
- Stack Sprawl
