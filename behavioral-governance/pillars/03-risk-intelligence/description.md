# Risk Intelligence

**"Not 'identify and manage risks' — that's structural and boring. What do you DO with risk data? And what do you do when you don't have it?"**

## The Claim

Risk Intelligence is dynamic risk sensing, not static risk registers. Traditional risk management catalogs known risks and assigns owners. Risk Intelligence asks harder questions: How quickly do we detect novel risks? What happens when the risk data is absent, ambiguous, or contradictory? How is frontline ethical practice — the everyday judgment calls people make about AI use — integrated into the governance cadence rather than siloed in a compliance function?

The dimension reframes risk from a noun (a register of items) to a verb (an organizational capability for sensing and responding). It addresses two governing conditions: Shadow AI Proliferation (risks that governance cannot see because the AI use is unauthorized) and Regulatory Volatility (the external risk landscape shifts faster than annual review cycles can accommodate). Organizations with mature Risk Intelligence do not merely manage known risks — they maintain institutional readiness for risks that have not yet materialized.

## Conditions

- Shadow AI Proliferation
- Regulatory Volatility
