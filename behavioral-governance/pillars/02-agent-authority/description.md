# Agent Authority

**"What does the AI get to decide on its own? What emails can it reply to? What transactions can it approve? This cannot be static."**

## The Claim

Agent Authority defines the boundaries of autonomous AI action — what an AI agent is permitted to do without human approval, what requires human-in-the-loop confirmation, and what is categorically prohibited. This is the dimension that governs machine autonomy directly, distinct from Decision Rights (which governs human authority over AI initiatives).

The critical insight is that agent authority cannot be static. As AI systems demonstrate reliability in bounded contexts, their authority envelopes should expand. As they encounter novel situations or edge cases, authority should contract. This requires continuous calibration — a governance cadence, not a governance document. The dimension sits at the intersection of complexity (AI capability is non-linear), velocity (the boundary of what agents can do shifts weekly), and the Three-Body Problem (the interaction between human intent, AI behavior, and organizational context produces emergent effects that no single actor controls).

## Conditions

- Complexity
- Velocity
- Three-Body Problem
