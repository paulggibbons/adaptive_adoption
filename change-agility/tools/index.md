# Change Agility Tools

Practitioner tools for assessing and building Change Agility capability. Each tool is designed to produce actionable output — a decision, a diagnosis, or a design input — not a report for its own sake.

## Available Tools

### COM-B Adoption Diagnostic
A structured diagnostic that assesses why adoption is stalling by identifying deficits in Capability, Opportunity, or Motivation. Produces intervention recommendations matched to the specific COM-B gap. Use when adoption rates are below expectations and the cause is unclear.

### Pillar Rapid Assessment
A lightweight self-assessment across the seven Change Agility pillars. Designed for workshop settings — 60 to 90 minutes with a cross-functional team. Produces a heat map of relative strengths and gaps across pillars. Use as an entry point before deeper assessment.

### Adoption Friction Mapper
A process-level tool that maps the specific friction points in an AI adoption journey — where users disengage, where workarounds emerge, where value leaks. Combines user journey mapping with behavioral observation. Use when adoption is technically successful but operationally underperforming.

### Readiness Sprint Canvas
A design tool for planning adoption sprints — short-cycle experiments that test adoption interventions before scaling. Includes hypothesis definition, success criteria, measurement approach, and decision rules. Use when moving from diagnosis to intervention.

### Stakeholder Influence Audit
An assessment of stakeholder dynamics affecting AI adoption: who enables, who blocks, who is affected but unheard. Goes beyond traditional stakeholder mapping by assessing behavioral indicators (what stakeholders *do*) rather than positional indicators (what role they hold). Use when adoption is politically complex.

### Trust Thermometer
A focused diagnostic assessing organizational trust as it relates to AI adoption. Measures perceived competence, benevolence, and integrity at the team level. Use when trust deficits — in the technology, in leadership, or in the governance framework — are suspected as adoption barriers.
