![Pillar 6 Model Card](assets/pillar-06-card.png)# Pillar 6: Prioritize Behavior

**Awareness, desire, and knowledge do not add up to action — change the environment and the behavior; the mindset catches up.**

---

## Why This Pillar Exists

The dominant failure mode in organizational change is what Paul Gibbons calls "persuade then pray": superb communication, education, and inspiration — followed by waiting for behavior to inevitably follow. It doesn't.

According to Harvard sociologist Frank Dobbin, hundreds of studies dating back to the 1930s suggest that antibias training does not reduce bias, alter behavior, or change the workplace. Ten percent of managerial training transfers back to the job. Persuasion produces results just as paltry.

The reason is the **intention-action gap** — the empirically documented chasm between what people intend to do and what they actually do. ADKAR's model — Awareness → Desire → Knowledge → Ability → Reinforcement — assumes this gap doesn't exist. Behavioral science proves it does. Aristotle named it *akrasia* (weakness of will) twenty-four centuries ago.

Pillar 6 is the evidence-based dismantling of awareness-first change. It replaces "persuade then pray" with a diagnosis-first, environment-first, behavior-first methodology.

---

## What It Replaces

| Old Approach | Pillar 6 |
|---|---|
| Awareness campaigns | Behavioral diagnosis |
| Training as the primary lever | Environment design |
| Values statements | Specific, observable behaviors |
| "Resistance" as diagnosis | Barrier analysis (COM-B) |
| One-size rollout | Pilot → measure → scale |
| Attitude change first | Behavior change first; attitude catches up |

## Intellectual Backdrop

- **Aristotle**, *Nicomachean Ethics*, Book VII — *akrasia*: the failure to act on one's own best judgment. The foundational account of the intention-action gap.
- **Hume, David**, *A Treatise of Human Nature* — "Reason is, and ought only to be, the slave of the passions." The philosophical origin of behavioral science.
- **Kahneman, Daniel**, *Thinking, Fast and Slow* — System 1 (fast, automatic, habitual) vs. System 2 (slow, deliberate, effortful). Most behavior is System 1. Training addresses System 2.
- **Thaler, Richard and Sunstein, Cass**, *Nudge* — Choice architecture: the environment shapes behavior more reliably than intention.
- **Michie, Susan et al.**, *The Behaviour Change Wheel* (2014) — COM-B model; taxonomy of 93 behavior change techniques (BCTs).
- **Meza, Robert and Gibbons, Paul**, "Behavioral Science Tools for the Change Professional," *The Future of Change Management, Vol. 1* (Phronesis Media, 2024) — the practitioner toolkit on which this pillar is operationally grounded.
- **Healy, James**, "Culture Change: Beyond Shared Values," *The Future of Change Management, Vol. 1* (2024) — "Change the environment, change the behaviour."

## Toolbox

### Tools (Diagnostic and Intervention)

| Tool | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Behavioral Systems Map** | Identify which parts of the system produce the behavior; direct effort toward highest-leverage points |
| **Behavior Specificity** | Define the observable action (who / what / when / where) rather than the vague outcome ("adoption") |
| **Four-Driver Diagnostic Model** (Gibbons) | Diagnose which of four quadrants is the primary blocker: Capability ("Can I?"), Motivation ("Why should I?"), Trust ("Should I?"), Opportunity/Friction ("Am I enabled or blocked?") |
| **Theoretical Domains Framework (TDF)** | Subdivide each driver for more precise intervention targeting |
| **Theory & Techniques (T&T) Tool** | Map diagnosed barriers to evidence-based behavior change techniques from Michie's 93-BCT taxonomy |
| **APEASE Framework** | Evaluate proposed interventions against: Acceptability, Practicability, Effectiveness, Affordability, Side effects, Equity |
| **EAST Framework** | Design interventions to make the behavior Easy, Attractive, Social, Timely |
| **TESTS Framework** | Build measurement in from the start: Target → Explore → Solution → Trial → Scale |
| **Sludge Audit** | Find the environmental friction that makes the desired behavior harder than it needs to be |
| **Nudge Dashboards** | Real-time usage feedback as behavioral signal |
| **A/B Testing Interventions** | Data-driven comparison of behavioral interventions before scaling |

### Process

**SHIFT Method** (Meza / Aim for Behavior):

1. **Specify the Behavior** — Translate vague outcomes ("adopt the system") into observable actions (who does what, when, where). Vagueness makes diagnosis impossible.
2. **Hypothesize (Diagnose Barriers)** — Use COM-B and TDF to find what's actually blocking the behavior: capability, environmental fit, or motivation. Don't assume resistance.
3. **Intervene** — Match the fix to the diagnosed barrier. The most common mistake is creating good strategies for the wrong barriers.
4. **Facilitate Stakeholders** — Apply the same diagnostic lens to the people who must approve, resource, or reinforce the intervention.
5. **Test and Iterate** — Pilot before scaling. Measure behavior, not sentiment. Adjust based on what people actually do, not what they say.

### Behaviors (What Practitioners Do)

- **Behavioral Pinpointing** — Specifics over vagueness; always define the observable action before designing the intervention
- **Habit Stacking** — Anchor new behaviors to established routines
- **ABC Analysis** — Map Antecedents → Behavior → Consequences to understand the behavioral system
- **Social Proofing** — "I saw leaders do it" — leverage descriptive norms and peer behavior
- **Environmental Design** — Tweak defaults, reduce friction, restructure choice architecture
- **Identity Re-framing** — "I am an AI director" — shift self-concept to support new behaviors
- **Self-Monitoring** — Track one's own behavioral progress as a feedback loop
- **Detox Cards** — Flag toxic compliance (going through the motions without the behavior)
- **Goal & Sub-goal Setting** — Micro-sprints rather than distant targets
- **Public Commitment** — Leverage social pressure through declared intention
- **Feedback Reception** — Data as guidance, not judgment

### Craft Skills

- **Behavioral Literacy** — Working fluency with COM-B, BCTs, intention-action gap, System 1/2 dynamics
- **If-Then Planners (Implementation Intentions)** — "If I encounter [situation], then I will [specific behavior]" — Gollwitzer's evidence-based approach to closing the intention-action gap
- **Barrier Interviewing** — Distinguishing capability barriers from motivation barriers from environmental barriers in practitioner conversations
- **Friction Mapping** — Identifying sludge (bureaucratic or environmental friction preventing the desired behavior)

## Leadership Delta

The single most important change a leader can make: **stop diagnosing resistance, start diagnosing barriers.**

When adoption stalls, the default leadership response is to assume motivation failure — and respond with more messaging, more training, more sponsorship. SHIFT asks first: Is this a capability problem? An environmental problem? A motivation problem? The intervention depends entirely on the answer. Leaders who skip diagnosis waste resources and create cynicism.

The second delta: **behavior-model before you behavior-manage.** Role modeling is the highest-leverage environmental intervention available to a leader. If senior leaders are not visibly using AI tools, no communication campaign will move the organization.

---

## Common Failure Modes

- Diagnosing "resistance" without barrier analysis — and responding with more communication
- Designing training for a capability barrier that is actually environmental
- Rolling out to the full organization before piloting and measuring behavior change
- Measuring sentiment ("Are you satisfied with the training?") instead of behavior ("Did the behavior happen? Did it sustain?")
- Treating COM-B as a one-time diagnosis rather than an iterative measurement
- Values-first culture change that assumes attitude drives behavior — it doesn't; behavior drives attitude
