# Change Agility — Provenance

## Layer Identity
**The Flywheel** — what the organization does. Seven pillars for building AI adoption capability through practice, complexity navigation, trust calibration, augmentation-first design, prototyping, behavioral science, and frontline ethics.

## Intellectual Lineage

| Tradition | Key Source | Contribution to This Layer |
|---|---|---|
| Behavioral Science | Michie et al., *Behaviour Change Wheel* (2014); Kahneman, *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (2011) | COM-B model, intention-action gap, System 1/2 dynamics |
| Complexity Theory | Snowden, Cynefin (1999); Stacey, *Complex Responsive Processes* (2001); Meadows, *Thinking in Systems* (2008) | Probe-sense-respond, nonlinear systems, leverage points |
| Organizational Development | Senge, *The Fifth Discipline* (1990); Schein, *Organizational Culture and Leadership* (2010) | Systems thinking, learning organizations, culture as shared assumptions |
| Design Thinking | Brown, *Change by Design* (2009); Ries, *The Lean Startup* (2011) | Prototyping, iteration, build-measure-learn |
| Trust Research | Mayer, Davis & Schoorman, integrative model (1995); Edmondson, psychological safety (1999) | ABI framework, undertrust/overtrust spectrum |
| Learning Science | Dewey, *Experience and Education* (1938); Wenger, *Communities of Practice* (1998); Schön, *The Reflective Practitioner* (1983) | Learning by doing, situated learning, knowing-in-action |
| Applied Ethics | Aristotle, *phronesis*; Rest, four-component model (1986); Gebru et al., model cards (2019) | Practical wisdom, ethical reasoning as skill, transparency tools |

## Gibbons Contributions
- Four-Driver Diagnostic Model (Capability, Motivation, Trust, Opportunity) — applied across all 7 pillars
- AI Mastery Matrix (6 dimensions × 4 levels) — Pillar 1
- Undertrust/overtrust spectrum — Pillar 3
- Augmentation-first sequencing — Pillar 4
- Ethics as practiced capability (not compliance) — Pillar 7
