# Adaptive Adoption: Two-Tier Framework
## Board Accountability + Operational System (7×5)

---

## The Category Break

**Change management was built for projects. AI is not a project. It's a permanent capability shift.**

Traditional change management implicitly depends on conditions that do not hold for enterprise AI:

- **Clear end state** — AI capability keeps moving
- **Clear timeline** — no stabilization phase; continuous iteration
- **Clear resourcing** — diffuse ownership; shadow AI; decentralized experimentation
- **Sufficient trust in the tech** — errors, hallucinations, bias, and privacy fears create fragility
- **Ethical neutrality** — AI is ethically "hot," not neutral
- **Turnkey solutions** — AI value is co-created; "deploy + train" rarely yields durable gains

---

## Competitive Landscape

### What everyone else is saying

The dominant pattern across McKinsey, Deloitte, and standards bodies frames enterprise AI as:
- **Governance/oversight** (risk, controls, ethics, compliance)
- **Operating model** (roles, workflows, ways of working)
- **Capability building** (skills, talent, training)
- **Value capture** (use cases, ROI, scaling)
- **Risk management frameworks** (trustworthiness and compliance alignment)

Packaged as maturity models or "AI journey stages," paired with a portfolio of services.

**McKinsey**: Boards aligning on AI posture, evolving oversight, management practices that correlate with value capture at scale. Net: treat AI as strategic and governance issue, not IT add-on.

**Deloitte**: Board governance roadmaps, "Trustworthy AI" concepts, governance structures mapped to broader frameworks. Net: board governance as repeatable oversight discipline.

**Standards bodies (NIST AI RMF, ISO/IEC 42001)**: Risk management infrastructure organized around GOVERN, MAP, MEASURE, MANAGE. Audit-friendly, process-evidence-focused.

### The opening

Even when competitors talk about "dashboards," the dominant emphasis is governance structures, policy controls, risk registers, maturity assessments, portfolio progress.

What is less consistently owned — and where the opening exists:

- **Behavioral** (deep utilization in real workflows, not policy compliance)
- **Trust-centric** (trust fragility and recovery as a measurable variable)
- **Capability-expansion-centric** (new options created, not just time saved)
- **Framed as a permanent capability regime**, not a project

Everyone can do maturity. Fewer can do "capability regime governance" with credible behavioral and trust mechanics.

## The Two-Tier Framework

### Top Layer: Board / C-Suite Accountability (Five Dials)

Not a maturity model. A governance dashboard that answers board questions:

- "Why is this going so slowly?"
- "Where's the uplift we were promised?"
- "Why is the P/L flat or negative after big spend?"
- "What risks are rising as we scale usage?"

**The Five Dials** (board-legible, falsifiable, tied to outcomes):

| # | Dial | What It Measures |
|---|------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Utilization Depth** | AI embedded into core workflows vs superficial tool use |
| 2 | **Capability Expansion Rate** | New options/products/workflows created (not only efficiency) |
| 3 | **Trust Stability** | Trust fragility and recovery, incident patterns, reversion rates |
| 4 | **Iteration Velocity** | Speed from experiment → safe deployment; learning rate |
| 5 | **Leadership Delta** | Observable leadership behavior shift, literacy, decision modernization |

### Bottom Layer: Adaptive Adoption Operational System (7×5)

Seven domains (the AA wheel) each operationalized via five implementation categories:

| Implementation Category | Description |
|------------------------|-------------|
| **Tools** | What systems and platforms enable this domain |
| **Processes** | What workflows and routines instantiate it |
| **Skills** | What capabilities people need |
| **Behaviors** | What observable actions are required |
| **Leadership Delta** | What leaders specifically must do differently |

The bottom layer is the "how we move the dials" machinery.

## Dial Operationalization

Each dial defined with indicators, data sources, and interpretation.

### Dial 1: Utilization Depth

**Definition**: The degree to which AI is embedded in core workflows rather than used superficially or sporadically.

| Indicator | Data Source |
|-----------|-------------|
| % of critical workflows AI-augmented (not "license assigned") | System logs, workflow audit |
| Repeated-use rate in target roles | Usage analytics |
| AI usage in "value moments" (customer interactions, decision points) | Workflow observation |
| Reversion rate to manual methods | Workflow audit, survey |

**If low**: Primary levers → Prioritize Behavior (behavior design, reinforcement loops), Design and Prototype (workflow redesign), Master the Craft (prompt literacy, role-based skill building)

---

### Dial 2: Capability Expansion Rate

**Definition**: The rate at which the organization creates net-new capabilities, offerings, or workflows enabled by AI — not just efficiency gains on existing work.

| Indicator | Data Source |
|-----------|-------------|
| # of net-new AI-enabled offerings/workflows shipped per quarter | Product/delivery tracking |
| Ratio of efficiency use cases to new capability use cases | Portfolio review |
| Time from idea → prototype → production | Project tracking |
| Pipeline conversion rate (experiment → scaled capability) | Experiment log |

**If stalled**: Primary levers → Design and Prototype (portfolio of experiments that ship), Embrace Complexity (move beyond "turnkey" thinking), Put People First (role evolution, human+AI job redesign)

---

### Dial 3: Trust Stability

**Definition**: The stability and calibration of trust in AI systems — tracking both overtrust and undertrust incidents, and the organization's recovery speed and learning from trust failures.

| Indicator | Data Source |
|-----------|-------------|
| Incident frequency (accuracy, privacy, bias) + severity | Incident register |
| Time-to-detect and time-to-resolve | Incident register |
| Trust pulse trend (not static "trust score") | Periodic survey |
| Post-incident usage drop and recovery time | Usage analytics |

**If fragile**: Primary levers → Consciously Manage Trust (incident response, transparency, evaluation practices), Manage Ethics Always (risk assessment, governance controls, policy clarity), Leadership Delta (leaders modeling responsible usage and disclosure discipline)

---

### Dial 4: Iteration Velocity

**Definition**: The speed at which the organization moves from experiment to deployment, and the rate at which it learns what works.

| Indicator | Data Source |
|-----------|-------------|
| Cycle time from experiment to controlled deployment | Project tracking |
| Governance throughput (reviews completed / backlog) | Governance logs |
| Prompt/workflow library evolution (rate of improvement) | Library versioning |
| Measurement cadence (how fast you learn what's working) | Review calendar |

---

### Dial 5: Leadership Delta

**Definition**: The observable change in leadership behavior — usage, literacy, and decision-making — as a leading indicator of organizational adoption depth.

| Indicator | Data Source |
|-----------|-------------|
| Leader usage visibility and regularity | Usage analytics, observation |
| AI literacy distribution in top team (variance matters) | Assessment |
| Decision cycle compression in leadership processes | Meeting audit |
| Quality of governance questions leaders ask | Board/ExCo observation |

## Dial-to-Lever Mapping

The causal story connecting board metrics to operational interventions.

| If This Dial Is Low/Fragile | Primary AA Levers |
|----------------------------|-------------------|
| Utilization Depth | Prioritize Behavior, Design and Prototype, Master the Craft |
| Capability Expansion Rate | Design and Prototype, Embrace Complexity, Put People First |
| Trust Stability | Consciously Manage Trust, Manage Ethics Always, Leadership Delta |
| Iteration Velocity | Design and Prototype, Master the Craft, Embrace Complexity |
| Leadership Delta | All 7 pillars — leadership is the multiplier, not a separate track |

This mapping is what makes the system different from generic maturity models: not just "where you are," but "what you do next and why."

## Minimum Viable Diagnostic Instrument (Beta)

### Assessment Components

- 20–35 questions across the five dials answered via:
  - Executive interviews
  - Evidence review (artifacts, policies, usage stats)
  - Short targeted survey where needed
  - Incident/governance logs
- One-page dashboard with dial scores and confidence levels
- 5–10 slide readout deck: "here's what's slowing you down and where to intervene"

### Beta Engagement Structure (Repeatable)

| Week | Activities |
|------|-----------|
| Week 0 | Scoping call + data request |
| Week 1 | Leadership interviews + artifact review + usage/incident data |
| Week 2 | Scoring + narrative synthesis + recommendations tied to levers |
| Week 3 | Executive readout + board-ready deck + 90-day action plan |

### Board Dashboard Output (Two Slides)

**Slide 1**: Five dials + trend arrows + risk notes
**Slide 2**: "Top 3 constraints slowing adoption" + recommended interventions

## Sequencing Strategy

**Board wedge first. Practitioner certification second.**

If certification comes first, the product falls back into the change-practitioner economy.

Instead:
1. Land 2–5 board/C-suite diagnostics
2. Extract patterns and case examples
3. Build practitioner certification as the delivery engine: "Certified Adaptive Adoption Practitioner" = implements the levers that move the dials

### After Beta

1. Refine the five dials based on real delivery feedback
2. Productize the dial → lever mapping (defensible IP)
3. Operationalize 7×5 as practitioner playbooks
4. Build training/certification once the top layer is credible in market
5. Publish selectively (briefings, keynotes) aimed at AI adopters and governance owners — not the change profession
