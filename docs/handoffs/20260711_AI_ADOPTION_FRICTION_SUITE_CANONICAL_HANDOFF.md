# AI Adoption Friction Suite — Canonical Product, Customer Journey, Course, and Build Handoff

## Purpose

This document fixes the product decisions made across the strategy conversation about the **AI Adoption Friction Suite**. It is the durable handoff for Codex desktop, Claude/Supabase work, Maven production, Zep memory, and Obsidian `/session-trace`.

No assistant chat is authoritative. This file and the repository artifacts it references are authoritative.

> Build a reusable three-tool intellectual product on Paul Gibbons Advisory. Use Maven to teach the method. Use the free Audit as the entry point, the paid course as the guided application layer, and the Mapper and Planner as the deeper operating system.

## Product thesis

AI adoption work often misdiagnoses stalled initiatives as generic resistance, communication failure, or lack of training. The Suite treats failure as an interaction among value, workflow, technology and data, capability, trust, incentives and identity, leadership and governance, and measurement and adaptation.

The unit of analysis is **one named AI initiative, deployment, use case, or human–AI workflow**. This is not another enterprise-wide maturity model.

Working promise:

> Diagnose what is blocking one AI initiative, identify where the friction is concentrated, and build a focused 90-day response.

## The four-step customer journey

### 1. Recognize the problem

Customer state: an AI pilot or initiative exists, but changed work and value are not materializing.

Entry mechanisms:

- Paul’s essays, posts, keynotes, and outreach;
- the course landing page;
- referrals from AI, transformation, PE, HR, and business leaders;
- an optional future free live Maven Lightning Lesson.

Core message:

> “Resistance” is often a label for workflow debt, legitimacy gaps, conflicting incentives, governance delay, or weak evidence.

Optional Lightning Lesson:

- **Why AI Adoption Gets Stuck**
- free and live;
- 30–45 minutes;
- awareness and demonstration, not the substantive paid product;
- built only after the Audit and course assets are stable.

### 2. Diagnose one initiative

Product: **AI Adoption Friction Audit**

Location: Paul’s website, not Maven.

Role: free public diagnostic and high-intent entry point.

Outcome:

- assess 32 conditions across eight domains;
- distinguish friction from consequence;
- identify the behavioral evidence layer;
- identify priority domains and evidence gaps;
- generate cross-domain hypotheses and first investigative moves.

The Audit must remain independently useful. A user should not need the course to understand the result.

Title: **Why Your AI Adoption Is Stuck**

Outcome: bounded initiative brief, completed Audit, defensible working diagnosis, evidence plan, and provisional 90-day response.

Maven hosts the learning experience. The live Audit remains on Paul’s website and is linked as the core course resource.

## The three tools

The tools share one taxonomy, evidence language, and data model, but answer different questions.

### Tool 1 — AI Adoption Friction Audit

**Question:** What kinds of friction are preventing this initiative from producing changed work and value?

**Unit:** one named initiative or workflow.

**Inputs:** 32 condition ratings; consequence and behavioral-evidence-layer ratings by domain; Unable to assess as missing information.

**Outputs:** eight-domain profile, top priorities, evidence gaps, cross-domain hypotheses, first-move prompts, and 90-day planning questions.

**Epistemic position:** Level 1 conceptual tool, informed by research and practice, not psychometrically validated.

It does not prove causality, certify maturity, assess legal or technical risk, measure personality, or replace interviews, observation, and operational evidence.

### Tool 2 — AI Adoption Friction Mapper

**Question:** Where is the friction concentrated, between which groups, and where does it propagate through the work?

**Unit:** the organizational system around the same initiative.

The Mapper is not the Audit averaged by department and not a decorative heatmap. It must capture:

- where friction is experienced, originates, and is amplified;
- who owns the decision and who absorbs the cost;
- where handoffs fail and workarounds emerge;
- where evidence is missing.

Initial views:

1. organization: function/team/role × friction domain;
2. journey: workflow/adoption stage × friction domain.

Do not force a three-dimensional team × stage × domain interface in v1.

### Tool 3 — 90-Day Intervention Planner

**Question:** What should be done next, by whom, in what sequence, and with what evidence?

**Unit:** the priority friction pattern produced by the Audit and Mapper.

Every intervention states:

- friction addressed;
- mechanism and rationale;
- responsible owner and affected groups;
- dependencies and timing;
- evidence required and expected behavioral signal;
- stop, adapt, continue, or scale checkpoint.

The output is a bounded 90-day plan, not a generic change-management checklist.

## Shared taxonomy and evidence model

Eight domains:

1. Value and strategic fit
2. Workflow fit
3. Technology and data fit
4. Capability and learning
5. Trust and legitimacy
6. Incentives, power, and professional identity
7. Leadership and governance
8. Measurement and adaptation

Do not merge these scales:

### Tool epistemic strength

1. Conceptual
2. Research-grounded
3. Face validity
4. Construct validity
5. Predictive validity

### Behavioral evidence layer

1. Anecdotal/reported/self-report
2. Artifact evidence
3. Observed/enacted behavior

A polished or commercially useful tool is not thereby validated. A conceptual tool may still gather observed evidence.

## The Maven course

### Canonical title

**Why Your AI Adoption Is Stuck**

### Canonical subtitle

**Use the Adaptive Adoption Friction Audit to diagnose what is blocking value and build a focused 90-day response.**

### Intended learner

AI transformation and adoption leaders; business sponsors; product and programme leaders; change, OD, and learning practitioners; consultants; and functional leaders redesigning human–AI work.

### Product-format decision

The conversation considered Maven’s one-day **Workshop** product, but the final decision was:

> Build this as a **Maven Course**, not a Workshop and not a Lightning Lesson.

Reasons:

- Workshops are positioned as paid one-day sprints and may imply live participation;
- Lightning Lessons are free and live;
- the core offer must be asynchronous, evergreen, and independent of Paul’s calendar;
- the experience needs a sequence, cases, tools, saved artifacts, and a workbook.

### One-day versus multi-day reconciliation

The learner experience is approximately **3–4 hours** and may be completed in one concentrated day. The canonical Maven structure is four modules, with four suggested days or self-paced completion.

Therefore:

- learner promise: a focused intensive that can be completed in one day;
- Maven product type: Course;
- pedagogical structure: four modules;
- no required schedule: one sitting or several days.

Do not describe it as a live one-day workshop unless the product strategy is deliberately changed.

### Course constraints

The course must be:

- fully asynchronous;
- evergreen and low-maintenance;
- independent of office hours, clinics, community, feedback, or cohort participation;
- anchored by short videos in Paul’s authentic voice;
- free of certification, psychometric, or personal-consulting promises.

### Four-module learner journey

#### Module 1 — Define the initiative and adoption problem

Outcome: bounded initiative brief.

Topics: why AI adoption is too broad; initiative, workflow, user group, intended value, and observed problem; adoption as changed work; symptoms versus assumed causes.

Saved artifact: initiative brief.

Paul video: 12–15 minutes across two short segments.

#### Module 2 — Diagnose the friction

Outcome: completed Audit.

Topics: eight domains; present conditions versus aspirations; friction versus consequence; anecdotal, artifact, and observed evidence; Unable to assess as an evidence gap.

Saved artifact: Audit result and evidence-gap list.

Paul video: 15–18 minutes across three short segments.

#### Module 3 — Interpret the pattern

Outcome: defensible working diagnosis.

Topics: no grand maturity score; domain interactions; rational and protective friction; alternatives; disconfirming evidence; training problem versus workflow, incentive, trust, or governance problem.

Saved artifact: interpretation and evidence plan.

Paul video: 15–18 minutes across three short segments.

#### Module 4 — Build the 90-day response

Outcome: provisional 90-day plan.

Topics: intervention-mechanism fit; ownership and decision rights; behavioral signals and evidence; dependencies; stop, adapt, continue, and scale checkpoints.

Saved artifact: 90-day planning canvas.

Paul video: 15–18 minutes across three short segments.

### Total course production

- learner experience: 3–4 hours;
- Paul recording: approximately 60–70 minutes total;
- multiple short anchor videos, not long lectures;
- cadence: idea → diagnosis → case → application;
- every module ends with a saved artifact and completion criterion.

### Course assets

1. initiative brief;
2. live website Audit;
3. interpretation guide;
4. three worked cases;
5. evidence-strength guide;
6. 90-day planning canvas;
7. printable workbook;
8. Maven-ready lesson copy;
9. Paul recording scripts;
10. upload/build checklist.

### Tool-course boundary

- Maven teaches the method.
- Paul’s website hosts interactive tools.
- GitHub holds canonical content and specifications.
- Link the Audit prominently from Maven.
- Embed later only if Maven supports it cleanly.
- Do not block the course on the Mapper or Planner; use worksheet versions initially.

- Product family: AI Adoption Friction Suite.
- Three distinct tools: Audit, Mapper, Planner.
- Audit unit: one initiative, not enterprise maturity.
- Mapper is not the Audit aggregated by department.
- Website hosts tools; Maven teaches the method.
- Paid offer is a Maven Course, not the Workshop product.
- The course is asynchronous, evergreen, and may be completed in one intensive day through four modules.
- Paul appears through short anchor videos, not required live delivery.
- Audit remains Level 1 and not psychometrically validated.
- Behavioral evidence and epistemic strength remain separate.
- Initiative narrative is not stored by default.
- Longitudinal access uses a private comparison key, not case label.
- Benchmark consent is separate; Audit minimum is `n = 5`.
- Course-ready Audit precedes Mapper; Mapper precedes Planner.
