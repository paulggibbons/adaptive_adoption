# AI Adoption Friction Audit — Item Bank v0.1

**Status:** provisional
**Scoring direction:** Higher ratings indicate healthier enabling conditions and therefore lower friction.

## Value and strategic fit

1. **V1.** The initiative addresses a clearly defined operational or strategic problem.  
   *Evidence prompt:* What evidence shows that the problem is consequential and currently exists?
2. **V2.** The intended outcome is specific enough to evaluate.  
   *Evidence prompt:* What baseline and target define improvement?
3. **V3.** Key sponsors agree on what success means for this initiative.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Where is agreement documented, and where do definitions differ?
4. **V4.** The initiative remains a real priority when resources or attention are contested.  
   *Evidence prompt:* What recent resource or tradeoff decisions demonstrate priority?

## Workflow fit

1. **W1.** The AI capability is embedded in a clearly defined workflow rather than offered as a separate optional tool.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Map the tasks immediately before and after AI use.
2. **W2.** Handoffs between people, AI systems, and other teams are explicitly designed.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Identify who receives, verifies, or acts on outputs.
3. **W3.** Users have sufficient time and authority to change the routines required by the initiative.  
   *Evidence prompt:* What old tasks stop, change, or transfer?
4. **W4.** Exceptions, failures, and low-confidence outputs have a workable handling process.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Review recent exceptions and escalation paths.

## Technology and data fit

1. **T1.** The data available to the AI system are adequate for the intended use.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Review missing, stale, biased, inaccessible, or poorly structured data.
2. **T2.** Intended users can access the system reliably in their actual work environment.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Observe access across devices, locations, permissions, and peak periods.
3. **T3.** System outputs are sufficiently reliable for the stated task under real operating conditions.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Compare production examples with accepted quality thresholds.
4. **T4.** Integration and support problems are resolved quickly enough to sustain use.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Examine support backlog, resolution time, and recurring issues.

## Capability and learning

1. **C1.** Users know when the AI system should and should not be used.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Ask users to classify appropriate, inappropriate, and uncertain cases.
2. **C2.** Users can evaluate outputs and detect important errors.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Use realistic examples to test verification and escalation behavior.
3. **C3.** Users have opportunities to practice and receive useful feedback.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Review practice tasks, coaching, feedback loops, and learning data.
4. **C4.** Managers and local experts can support learning when problems arise.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Identify who can answer workflow, policy, and judgment questions.

## Trust and legitimacy

1. **R1.** Users understand enough about system strengths and limitations to calibrate reliance.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Ask how users decide to accept, verify, override, or escalate an output.
2. **R2.** Material concerns about privacy, fairness, safety, or professional standards are addressed.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Review unresolved concerns, controls, incidents, and responsible owners.
3. **R3.** The implementation process visibly respects the expertise of affected users.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Identify where practitioner knowledge changed the design or rollout.
4. **R4.** People can report failures, workarounds, or doubts without penalty.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Look for near-miss reporting, challenge behavior, and suppressed bad news.

## Incentives, power, and professional identity

1. **I1.** Expected adoption behaviors are consistent with performance measures and rewards.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Compare stated expectations with targets, incentives, and promotion criteria.
2. **I2.** The initiative does not impose substantial uncompensated work on intended users.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Estimate learning, verification, correction, and reporting burden.
3. **I3.** Changes to roles, authority, and accountability are explicit.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Map who gains or loses discretion, visibility, and decision rights.
4. **I4.** Affected groups can identify a credible benefit for themselves or their work.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Ask each major group who benefits, how, and when.

## Leadership and governance

1. **G1.** A clearly accountable owner has authority to resolve cross-functional barriers.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Identify decisions the owner can make without escalation.
2. **G2.** Decision rights for product, workflow, risk, and adoption questions are clear.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Test recent contested decisions and who resolved them.
3. **G3.** Leaders respond consistently when delivery pressure conflicts with responsible use.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Examine recent tradeoffs involving speed, quality, risk, or human impact.
4. **G4.** Escalation and governance processes operate at the speed required by the initiative.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Compare decision-cycle time with the initiative’s learning and operating cycle.

## Measurement and adaptation

1. **M1.** The initiative has a credible baseline for the outcome it intends to improve.  
   *Evidence prompt:* What was measured before deployment?
2. **M2.** Measures distinguish tool activity from changed work and actual outcomes.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Separate access, usage, task behavior, quality, and value measures.
3. **M3.** User feedback, operational data, and incidents lead to visible changes in the initiative.  
   *Evidence prompt:* Trace recent evidence to a product, workflow, training, or governance change.
4. **M4.** Leaders have explicit rules for when to stop, adapt, continue, or scale.  
   *Evidence prompt:* What evidence would trigger each decision?
